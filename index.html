03 / Hero strategy

Variation-level analysis

Analyzing performance at the ASIN level is misleading because SportsTrail uses a Hero strategy - ad spend is concentrated on a few key ASINs (heroes) that drive traffic to the entire variation. Shoppers arrive through the hero ASIN but purchase other children. This makes the hero ASIN appear unprofitable, even though the entire variation generates profit.

07 / Conclusions and opportunities

What works and where to improve

What is working well

1. Conversion is the primary growth driver. Not more traffic, not more ad spend - simply better-converting listings. Portfolio-wide conversion increased from 9.0% to 13.2%.

2. Hero strategy concentration is paying off. At the full variation level: daily profit +166%, TACoS -8.8 p.p., every $1 of ad spend now generates $2.33 in profit (was $1.41).

3. Customers are choosing higher-priced products. Average price per unit increased from $15.59 to $17.31. The $15.99 and $19.99 segments are growing fastest and most profitably.

4. Non-Slip is the profit foundation. 53.7% margin, 1.7% TACoS, +66% daily revenue growth. It accounts for 47% of total portfolio profit with minimal advertising.

Where to improve

1. B08HNC4881 ($19.99) - $4 673 in ad spend over 22d, but only 3.4% margin (ACOS 49%). If this ASIN is not a hero in its variation - that is potentially ~$4K that could be reallocated more efficiently.

2. B07C4YJYY2 ($10.99) - former second hero, ad spend now reduced, but margin is still -0.3%. Worth evaluating whether it still serves a hero function, or if the budget can be further consolidated into the primary hero.

3. 4 new ASINs ($19.99, "Name not found") - B0F6D7NR7J, B0F6D7W9B9, B0F6D92MGD, B0F6DCDPMR. Ad spend of $591, zero sales. If this is a new product launch - progress should be monitored.

Key takeaway

March 2026 demonstrates a fundamental efficiency improvement: -25% sessions, -10% ad spend, yet +71% revenue and +108% profit. This is not a seasonal or market effect - it is the result of portfolio optimization and Hero strategy execution. Every ad dollar now works 39% more efficiently than last year.
